VAT (Value-Added Tax)

The Value-Added Tax (VAT) is a type of sales tax that is imposed on the consumption of goods, whether sold, exchanged, bartered, or leased in the Philippines. As an indirect tax, it can be transferred or shifted to the purchaser, transferee, or lessee of the goods, properties, or services in question.


Who are Required to File VAT Return?


- Individuals or entities engaged in trade or business are subject to VAT if the total actual gross sales from rendered services exceed Three Million Pesos (Php 3,000,000.00).

- An individual who is required to register as a VAT taxpayer but fails to do so.

- Any individual, regardless of whether it is related to his or her business, who imports goods.




Percentage Tax / Non VAT

- The Percentage tax, also known as NonVAT, is a type of business tax that is levied on persons, entities, or transactions specified in Sections 116 to 127 of the National Internal Revenue Code of 1997 (also known as the Tax Code), as amended, and as required under special laws.


Who are required to file?


- Individuals who are not registered for VAT, and whose annual gross sales and/or receipts from the sale of goods, properties, or services do not exceed three million pesos (Php3,000,000.00) are exempt from value-added tax (VAT).

- Individuals who lease residential units with a monthly rental exceeding fifteen thousand pesos (Php15,000.00) per unit, but whose aggregate rental income for the year does not exceed three million pesos (Php3,000,000.00).




Withholding Tax

It can be defined as a fixed amount of income tax that an employer deducts from an employee's paycheck.


Importance of Withholding Tax System

- The promotion of voluntary compliance is encouraged.
- It lowers the expenses associated with collection efforts.
- It helps to avoid delinquencies and loss of revenue.
- It helps to prevent fiscal instability by providing a steady stream of revenue throughout the taxable year.




VAT Relief

The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) provides a program or software called Reconciliation of Listing for Enforcement (RELIEF) that is utilized to submit Quarterly Summary Lists of Sales and Purchases (SLSP), which is a mandatory attachment to BIR Form.


When should I file VAT relief?

Taxpayers are required to submit the applicable quarterly summary list of sales or purchases to the Revenue District Office (RDO), Large Taxpayers District Office (LTDO), or Large Taxpayers Assistance Division (LTAD) with jurisdiction over them on or before the 25th day of the month following the end of each taxable quarter.

Mayors Business Permit

The Mayor's Permit is a document issued by a local government unit's mayor, allowing a business to operate within their jurisdiction.

To operate a business in the Philippines, it is necessary to obtain a Mayor's Permit or Business Permit from the Local Government Unit (LGU) where the company office is located. LGUs can be cities or municipalities and the procedures for obtaining a permit may vary depending on the local regulations and ordinances that the city or municipality seeks to implement.



PCAB Licensing

The PCAB license issued by the Philippine Contractors Accreditation Board is intended to promote excellence and competence among contractors in their operations. Its aim is to ensure that all contractors possess the necessary skills and qualifications to undertake construction of infrastructure projects. With a PCAB license, clients can be assured that the contractor they hire has a proven track record of meeting industry standards and regulations.


Who needs PCAB license?

The Philippine Contractors Accreditation Board (PCAB) license aims to ensure excellence and competence in contracting operations. It is mandated by Republic Act 4555, also known as the Contractor's License Law, and requires contractors who engage in the construction industry or any business of contracting in the Philippines to have a PCAB license. Holding a PCAB license ensures that contractors have qualified and masterful skills necessary to conduct constructions of infrastructure.

How do PCAB categorize and classifies contractors?

The PCAB license categorizes contractors based on their contracting capacity, which is determined by the allowable range of contract cost.
